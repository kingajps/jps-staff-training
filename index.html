SECTION 01 OF 07

Why This Matters

JPS is not registered under the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 — HMRC confirmed our deregistration following adoption of a cashless policy. However, every JPS staff member remains personally subject to the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA 2002) and the Terrorism Act 2000. These apply to everyone, not just regulated firms.

Critical

Ignorance is not a defence. A senior instruction to look the other way is not a defence. Your personal liability exists regardless of what you were told.

SECTION 02 OF 07

What Financial Crime Looks Like in Our World

Buyer-side — Money Laundering

Cash Conversion

Bidder pays via cash-deposited transfers and re-sells lots to convert into clean assets. JPS doesn't accept cash — but source-of-funds still matters.

Asset Value Storage

Bidder buys high-value lots with unexplained funds, holds them, then re-sells as "personal property."

Trade-Based Laundering

Lots exported to associated parties at inflated or deflated prices to move value across borders.

Nominee Bidding

Bidder uses another person's account to conceal beneficial ownership of purchases.

Seller-side — Money Laundering

Selling Stolen Goods

Vendor consigns goods they don't lawfully own, using JPS to legitimise disposal and channel funds.

Hidden Third-Party Interests

Vendor consigns goods subject to undisclosed HP, finance lease, or ROT — pockets proceeds before the true owner can intervene.

Proliferation Financing

Funding weapons of mass destruction programmes. Treat as the most serious category. Signs: customer exporting to sanctioned countries, dual-use machinery, evasiveness about end-use. Escalate immediately to MLRO.

SECTION 03 OF 07

Red Flags — What Raises Suspicion

You don't need certainty to escalate. The threshold is reasonable grounds to suspect — enough to make a reasonable person curious. Report it and let the MLRO decide.

  • Bidder reluctant to verify identity — CDD obstruction
  • Bidder asking to bid through someone else's account — nominee bidding
  • Bidder wants to pay cash or cryptocurrency
  • Bidder wants to pay more than hammer + premium
  • Bidder wants invoice in someone else's name — beneficial ownership concealment
  • Vendor unwilling to provide source-of-goods evidence
  • Vendor in a hurry — avoiding scrutiny
  • Vendor proceeds to offshore or unrelated party account
  • Lots being purchased for export to high-risk or sanctioned country — potential proliferation financing
  • PEP without source-of-funds explanation
  • Someone offers you a personal benefit in connection with your role — potential bribery, report immediately
Tipping Off — Criminal Offence

Once a SAR has been made or is anticipated, never tell a customer that checks are being made, that a report has been filed, or why a transaction is delayed. Say only: "We are still completing our checks." Maximum penalty: 2 years' imprisonment.

SECTION 04 OF 07

Your Obligations

Customer Due Diligence

Every Bidder and Vendor must have CDD documentation in place. Do not accept bids, issue invoices, or accept Vendor instructions unless CDD is complete or the MLRO has given express authority.

The risk classification in BidPath is set by the MLRO only. Never change a risk class without explicit MLRO instruction.

BidPath FlagWhat You Do
StandardStandard handling. Routine engagement.
High RiskStop. Do not progress bids, invoices, or instructions without written MLRO authorisation.
Lower Risk / SDD (Vendors only)Simplified onboarding. Standard handling thereafter — monitoring still applies.

Bribery

Gifts and gratuities are governed by the JPS Gifts Policy. Gifts up to £50 may be accepted without approval. Gifts above £50 require written justification and director sign-off. Log all gifts over £10 in the Gifts & Hospitality Register. Any gift connected with a specific instruction — by wording or timing — must be referred to the MLRO before acceptance. Operational staff (e.g. porters) may accept customer gratuities for assistance beyond normal duties; these are not subject to the conflict-of-interest threshold as operational staff hold no decision-making role, but should be logged where practicable.

Record-Keeping

All AML records are retained for a minimum of 5 years. Never delete or alter AML records independently. Default action: archive or flag for MLRO review.

How to Escalate

1

Stop

Do not continue processing the transaction.

2

Note

Write down what you observed — factual, not interpretive. Date, time, name(s), what you saw or heard.

3

Escalate

Contact the MLRO immediately by phone, followed by a written message.
